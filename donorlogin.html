DONOR

A blood donor is someone who voluntarily gives their blood to help save lives. Donors play a vital role in the healthcare system by providing the blood needed for emergencies, surgeries, and medical treatments. They must meet specific health criteria to ensure their blood is safe for use. Regular blood donation helps maintain an adequate supply in blood banks. This act of generosity can directly impact the lives of patients in critical need, A blood donor is an individual who willingly gives their blood to support those in medical need. Blood donors are the foundation of the donation system, ensuring a continuous supply for emergencies, surgeries, and chronic illnesses. They undergo health screenings to confirm they are eligible to donate, ensuring safety for both themselves and the recipients. Regular blood donation has benefits for donors too, such as health check-ups and a sense of fulfillment from helping others. Each donation can potentially save multiple lives, making donors true unsung heroes in society.
